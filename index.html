Intro

Hi there! I am Daniel Jurjo, but most of the people call me Jurjo; the spelling is something like /'xur.xo/ -thanks Anxo for the info-. I studied maths at the Complutense University of Madrid and then I move to Computer Science pursuing a Msc in Formal Methods.

Currently I am working as reasearch intern at the IMDEA Software Institute under the supervision of Jose F. Morales in the Madrid Flight on Chip project. We aim to specificate oracles in order to perfom formal testing of critical software in aerospacial industry. I am also interested in static analysis of programs and I am working in different approaches to try to capture errors due to floating point computations using different abstract domains as part of mi master thesis.

I am also interested in bioinspired algorithms, more concrete in applications of genetic algorithms over grammars. Right now I am learning a little bit about "functional" inductive logic programming. I would like to explain what it is about but I think I do not know it yet

I know a little about a lot of things and I spend most of my time adding topics to the list. You can check out my curriculum vitae if you want to know what things I am supposed to know.

I will update this in the future adding more stuff and, who knows maybe I can write some kind of blog so we can pretend we are in 2006. But right know you can find a bunch of topics I found interesting here.
